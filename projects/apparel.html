02 / Apparel

FUHE
Future Heritage

An apparel collection combining classic silhouettes and construction methods with modern materials and hardware. Three jackets that blend the familiar and the unknown.

Year
Fall 2025
Duration
10 Weeks
Methods
Pattern Making, Sewing
Context
Apparel Studio

Overview

FUHE stands for Future Heritage. The idea behind the collection was to take construction methods and silhouettes from older workwear traditions and rebuild them with modern materials and details. I wanted the jackets to feel like they could have existed in the past but clearly do not belong there.

I designed and made all three jackets from scratch, including the patterns. Each one has its own reference point and its own personality, but they work as a group.

Jacket 0001

Shoal

Made from 10oz grey ripstop fabric. Inspired by mechanic jackets from the 1970s and 80s. The Shoal jacket has large swooping pockets and triangle cutout details on the cuffs and right pocket. A woven backtab sits at the center back hem.

Jacket 0002

Knox

Made from 9oz green ripstop fabric. Inspired by 1960s military fatigues. The Knox jacket has a wide collar, a D-ring buckle on the left side, and a stenciled "49" graphic on the back. A woven houndstooth backtab finishes the hem.

Jacket 0003

Trestle

Made from 12oz cotton denim in a hickory stripe. Inspired by type 1 trucker jackets. The Trestle jacket has a front patch pocket on the right with a magnetic closure, a wide open back pocket on the left, and a denim backtab. The magnetic pocket was the detail I was most interested in exploring from a making standpoint.

Process

I did all the pattern making myself, which is where most of the design decisions happen for me. Getting the silhouette right on paper before cutting into fabric saves a lot of material. I sew on industrial machines, which makes a real difference in seam quality and how fast you can work.

The magnetic pocket on the Trestle jacket was the most technically interesting part of the collection. Getting the magnets to sit flush inside the denim without creating a visible bump took a few tries. The attraction strength also had to be calibrated so the pocket felt satisfying to close but was not difficult to open one-handed.
