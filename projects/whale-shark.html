Whale Shark
Vacuum

A vacuum concept that borrows its form language from the whale shark. A study in biomimicry and CMF.

Year
Winter 2024
Duration
2 Weeks
Context
CAD Class
Focus
Biomimicry, CMF
Hero image

Overview

This project came out of a CAD class where I was learning Rhino 8 for the first time. The brief was to design and render a vacuum cleaner. I was interested in using the project as an opportunity to explore biomimicry, so I started looking at the whale shark as a reference specifically the way its wide filter-feeding mouth passively collects material as it moves forward, which felt like a natural analogy for a vacuum.

The goal was to find the underlying logic of the animal and let that drive the form rather than just copying the surface. The result is a vacuum that reads as biomimetic without being literal about it.

Form and CMF

The form is low and wide with a broad intake at the front that references the whale shark's mouth. The body tapers toward the back in a way that suggests forward motion even when the object is sitting still. The proportions came from studying the fish from above, which gave the clearest read of that tapered silhouette.

For CMF I was interested in the spotted pattern of the whale shark skin. Rather than printing a pattern onto the surface I explored how the texture could be built into the material itself through a combination of matte and gloss finishes in the same color family. This kept the reference subtle and made the object feel considered rather than themed.

Rendering

I modeled this in Rhino and rendered in KeyShot. The rendering process was important for exploring CMF options since the material decisions are hard to evaluate in a CAD viewport. Being able to switch between finishes and lighting setups quickly in KeyShot is where a lot of the final material decisions got made.
